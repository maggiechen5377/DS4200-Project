Global Sales Distribution by Platform

// About This Chart

This box plot visualizes the sales distribution of video games across different platforms, sorted by median sales. Each box shows the interquartile range (Q1 to Q3) with a line at the median. Whiskers extend to the farthest non-outlier value, and coral dots mark individual outliers. Hover over any box or outlier dot for detailed statistics.

// Key Takeaway

Older platforms like the SEGA Genesis, NES, and Game Boy show notably higher median sales per game compared to modern consoles. This is likely because those platforms had far fewer titles overall, so individual games faced less competition and captured larger audience shares. Conversely, newer consoles such as the Xbox One and PlayStation 4 have much lower median sales per game, reflecting a heavily saturated market where consumer attention is spread across thousands of titles.
